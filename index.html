Nebula Hack

6 Hours of learning and competing

Hack a solution for a revolutionary idea
About Us

At HaZe, Our goal is to ensure that all talent has a chance to grow not only from within but also
prosper externally through revolutionary projects in a way that encourages creativity and innovation.
Thus we created Nebula Hack, a platform where students can showcase their problem solving skills by
coming up with creative solutions to real world problems.

Who can join?

Undergraduate Student developers and designers are encouraged to register and show off their developing skills
in solving real world problems. The participating teams will create a solution for a revolutionary idea during
the competition and present it to the judging panel towards the end of the competition.

This isn’t a time to just build something cool but also to learn. So undergraduate engineering students
from all levels are welcome to register as a team, learn and grow as a team and have a great time at the competition..!!

Rules

  1. Attendees from all backgrounds, genders and geographies are welcome.
  2. Each team is allowed to have one advisor. However, advisors are not allowed to
    participate in the competition. They may however assist the teams during the competition.

  1. A team should consist of at least two members and not more than 5 members.
  2. Undergrad engineering students from any major are welcome to register as teams.
    Each team member should have a valid college ID. Cross field teaming up is encouraged.
  3. Changes to team members cannot be made after 26th August, 2020. Please
    contact the Nebula Hack Committee in case of any last minute changes.
    The contact information can be found on our website.

  1. The event will be organized online via zoom due to COVID-19 situation
  2. It is the team’s responsibility to maintain social distancing and have protective equipment if
    the team decides to attend the competition. If the team members decide to meet for the purpose of
    this competition, Nebula Hack highly recommends the team members to take appropriate precautionary
    measures for COVID-19 such as social distancing, wearing face masks and taking precautionary measures.
  3. The zoom invitation will be sent out to the teams on the 26th of August, 2020. If a team has registered and
    no invitation is received, it is the team’s responsibility to reach out to the Nebula Hack committee via email.

  1. The theme for the competition shall be revealed an hour before the competition via email.
    This will be done to maintain fairness between the teams during the competition.
  2. Any software development tools or programming language can be used for the competition.
  3. To ensure fairness in the competition, all coding shall be created during the competition.
  4. Teams are permitted to use publicly developed and openly licensed API’s and SDKs for your project.
  5. Assets, SDKs, APIs or other tools or components available under a trial/free license may be used.
  6. Each team can only submit one entry for the competition. Participation at the event is subjected
    on a “per-team” basis meaning you are not allowed to be on more than one team at the event.
  7. A team may not code applications that violate the code of conduct. For example, Racially insensitive
    ideas for an application will automatically be disqualified. The applications created during the event
    shall not pose/portray any sort of disrespectful ideas.

  1. All teams should have a name and be registered with the GitHub community.
  2. All project development must be uploaded in the GitHub repository (public) before
    the competition ends. The time stamp in the repository shall be periodically checked and
    analyzed by the judges. All teams are responsible to submit their github repository names to
    the organisers within an hour after the competition starts.
  3. Final changes should be committed to the github repository on/ before August 29th, 2020 at 5pm.
    All GitHub repositories at the end of the competition on August 29th, 2020 at 5 pm. Failure to submit
    will result in disqualification. The team repository should be regularly updated during the event.
  4. Team repositories submitted successfully shall be reviewed at the end of the competition.
    Rule violations may lead to disqualifications of the team.
  5. All project submissions will be randomly code-reviewed to ensure the code is original work
    created at the event. Be mindful to update your repositories regularly. A single GitHub repository
    update towards the end of the competition will draw the suspicion that the work was not completed during the event.
  6. After the competition ends, the team leads will confirm with the judging board for a brief review
    of their submission and to confirm the team’s GitHub page has been correctly updated.

  1. Nebula Hack is dedicated in providing a harassment free experience for everyone regardless
    of age, gender, gender identity and expression, disability, physical appearance, body size,
    race, ethnicity, nationality, religion and computing experience.
  2. We do not tolerate disrespect of event participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery
    are not appropriate for any hackathon venue or can be used in the application development.
    Violating these rules will result in disqualification.
